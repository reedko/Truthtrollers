Why this reads as a knowledge graph

Typed things

Different shapes and colors signal that a case is not a claim, a claim is not a source claim, and a source is not just another box.

Typed relationships

Support, refute, nuance, authorship, and publication are visually distinct. The edges carry semantics, not just adjacency.

Hub structure

One source can emit many source claims. One case can contain many claims. Evaluation becomes a graph-derived state, not a detached score widget.

Case-centric investigation graph Case claims fan into source claims, which roll up into sources and metadata nodes.

Why this fits your product

Your platform already has graph-native ingredients. The real leap is treating source claims as first-class nodes, because that is where the evidence semantics live.
