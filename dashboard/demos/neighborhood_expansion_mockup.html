What “expand neighborhoods” means

A node’s neighborhood is the set of nodes directly connected to it. Clicking a node reveals its immediate relationships first, then maybe a second hop. Think of it like pulling one thread in a spiderweb and watching only the nearby strands light up.

Click Case → show Case Claims
Click Case Claim → show linked Source Claims
Click Source Claim → show Source + Evaluations
Click Author → show all Sources by that Author
Case node clicked Show the case’s claims. Hide or dim unrelated authors, publishers, users, and evaluations.
Case Claim clicked Expand outward to only the source claims connected to that one claim, plus their sources. This gives the “evidence chain” view without graph soup.
Source clicked Expand to author, publisher, and source claims contained in that source. Great for credibility and provenance mode.
User or Evaluation clicked Expand to the links/evaluations made by that user, which claims they touched, and what sources they favored.
Why do it this way? Because dumping the whole universe at once turns the graph into spaghetti during a windstorm. Neighborhood expansion keeps the view readable and gives the user a controlled sense of discovery.
